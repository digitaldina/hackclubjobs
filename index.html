About Hack Club

Hack Club is a network of the most technically talented teenagers in the US and world. Everything at Hack Club is free and open source (3,300 commits at GitHub), including our code and our financing. Check out "transparency mode" at Hack Club Bank (we built this) which lets the public see our salaries and all our spending.

Currently, Hack Club has nearly 500 computer science clubs around the country and world started by students in their own high schools, from Sarasota Florida, to Grand Isles, VT to Cincinnati, Ohio to Oakland, California.

10,000 students are on our Slack; and more than 100 CS events like hackathons (now virtual) happen each year. In 5 years, we hope Hack Club will have helped create a new generation of leaders with 21st century skills, as well as a mindset that values kindness, optimism, fun, creativity and passion for building great things that push progress.

Hack Club was founded in 2014 by Zach Latta, then a 14 year old programmer and high school dropout. Our average employee age currently is 22. We've created a space where students want to learn to build cool stuff that interests them with their friends- and they get hooked into the beauty and excitement of building with technology, and become self taught.

Job descriptions

Clubs lead

ABOUT: Afterschool student led computer science clubs (Hack Clubs) are at the heart of Hack Club, and we need someone to lead them. Through clubs, your job will be to create a new social identity (the hacker) across the world by making Hack Clubs a great experience.

QUALIFICATIONS: We are looking for a product person; who will own the student club experience from start to finish and they need to care about that deeply. This is the clubs game designer. This person will ask themselves every day- how do we make Hack Clubs the best after-school clubs out there? They need to understand weakness of our current clubs and strategize around how to improve. They will be on the phone with students a lot. Super detail oriented and really sweat the small stuff.

Mature and professional enough to set and meet targets, have great communication skills and work closely with leadership team. A highly technical,good team manager, that is professional at the level of leadership or ambitious to get there. This person will have to work with the team to grow awareness. They would have likely 3 direct staffer reports and 4 students.

Ideally we're looking for a great female hacker who known what it's like to be a student in an American public school.


Head of Community/Slack

ABOUT: Hack Club Slack is our organization’s circulatory system and keeping it vibrant, wholesome and technical is the goal of the Head of Community.

QUALIFICATIONS: This person would be in charge of running the moderation of Slack, addressing problems and moving on opportunities.

They must be great at listening and responding to students. They’re working closely with the events person to ensure events are always happening.

They are joyful and kind and patient and fun. They also run our Twitter. This candidate must be technical and must be a great at at least one modern programming language, Rust, Go, Python, Ruby etc.

They should already be recognized as having a following amongst young hackers, and shown an interest in educating and working with young people.

We're looking for someone who is ideally creative, fun energy, team player, cool person, little edgy, good communication skills, gets along well with others,and we’re biasing towards under 25.


Events Producer

ABOUT: Hack Club events creates a great path for students coming into the Hack Club world.

QUALIFICATIONS: This person runs all of our online events and makes sure they are compelling enough that students are always attending. They build out our “Ask-me-Anythings” and brings forth othe weird ideas that attracts new students and builds community.

Hack Club events can be weird, surprising, engaging, they are not overly pre-planned, and we have figured out how to pierce the PR-speak and ask questions that get to the heart of the guest’s world-view and lived experience.

This candidate should have some technical experience in putting on events or be willing to quickly learn them. They should be a cool, fun person, who understands how to engage a live audience, and is a talented storyteller who understands how to do compelling visual and audio events.
